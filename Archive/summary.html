Is there meaning in life?
Something beyond its end?

Film Summary

Helmed by Soroush Sehat, "Jahan Ba Man Beraghs" (Dance with Me) narrates a powerful drama of finding meaning in life and learning to accept its inevitable truths. The film tells the story of Jahangir, who suffers from a terminal illness, in his last days. For such a milestone in his life, Jahangir arranges a special birthday celebration at a villa set amidst nature and invites his closest friends to share this time with him.

However, this gathering is much more than just a celebration or a time to lament. The film explores the complexities of human relationships, focusing on the unsolved disputes, unacknowledged grievances, and personal problems that each of his friends bring to the table. This meeting allows the characters to confront the truths of life, reconcile their differences, and find the great value that human connections can offer to life.

Story Overview

Soroush Sehat's debut feature film "Jahan Ba Man Beraghs" follows Jahangir (Ali Mosaffa), a man with a fatal disease. Jahangir chooses to accept his situation and invites his old friends to a remote villa for a unique birthday celebration. Each buddy brings their own emotional baggage, unresolved concerns, and personal challenges, transforming the meeting into an opportunity to reflect on life and human connections.

The film switches its focus from Jahangir's death to the consequences of his condition for his friends and himself. The characters, despite their differences and unresolved pasts, are given the opportunity to reflect on the fundamental meaning of life and relationships. Some of their disputes remain unresolved, but the gathering offers them a rare moment of clarity and connection.
